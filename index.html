Technology, law, and policy

6.805: Foundations of Internet Policy

In one of my favorite courses from college, my group members and I wrote a final paper analyzing how fake news has affected American politics, examining efforts made by private and public entities to curb its prevalence. We make recommendations on how to mitigate fake news with a particular emphasis on implementing a hypothetical encrypted system that would verify users’ identities, and discuss its implications on free speech and expression. A special thank you to Prof. Danny Weitzner for this incredible experience.

Privacy Legislation: Law and Technology

In a joint course with MIT and Georgetown Law School, my group members and I wrote the Broadband ISP Privacy Act of 2018, an act that fosters competition among Internet Service Providers (ISPs) and provides consumers with meaningful choice about the collection and use of their private information. The end-of-semester project culminated in presenting our legislative proposal to a panel of public policy experts in Washington, D.C. You can view the presentation and technical demonstration, in which I was more heavily involved, here.

Relevant projects and experience

Polarization on Twitter

In this project, I examine how millennials are interacting on Twitter in the polarized political arena of today in the months leading up to the 2016 presidential election. I accomplish this by scraping the conversations of millennial journalists and visualizing their conversations using LDA topic modeling. (Languages/software used: Python, Selenium, sklearn)

Gen Z Critics

I created and maintain a website for an organization started by my sister. Gen Z Critics is a platform where youth can connect about their passion for film criticism. I onboard new writers, publish new reviews, and create author profiles. Instead of using a hefty framework like Angular that is heavily dynamic and dependent on user interaction, I wrote my own Python script for streamlining the process of publishing new content for this mostly static site (it is viewership-heavy rather than user interaction-heavy), so the process is mostly automated at this point.

Braintree

I interned as a software engineer at Braintree Payments, a subsidiary of Paypal and a leading company in the online payments space, when I was a sophomore in college. Using Ruby on Rails, I ensured the payment Gateway’s forward compatibility with Elasticsearch 5.x. I also worked with a Rewinder that would catch data inconsistencies between output from Kafka, a data storage platform, to the Elasticsearch loader. I see this experience as my stepping stone to understanding how software companies operate.

Up House

A fun recreation of one of the iconic scenes from Up, the 2009 Pixar film, which was the final project for my computer graphics course. I use fundamental CG concepts such as hierarchical modeling, directional light, Phong shading, texture-mapping, Bezier curves, and animation in rendering the scene. (Languages/software used: JavaScript, Three.js)
